A statement of the overall goal / research question

Our research question is “What is Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) and how has it changed over the years across countries?”

What is Purchasing Power Parity?

  • PPP stands for Purchasing Power Parity
  • It is a measurement used in economics to compare the purchasing power of different currencies in common units.
  • In theory, PPP should be equal to the exchange rate of these two countries, but in practice this is not the case.

The Big-mac Index Data

The dataset consists of 1,520 observations (rows) and 19 variables (columns).

Each row represents a country at a specific time (year-month-date) while the columns represents crucial information related to the countries.

The data is accountable for the years 2000-2021.

The Big Mac prices are from McDonald’s, and the exchange rates are from Thomson Reuters. The GDP per person data is from the IMF World Economic Outlook reports.

Why Big Macs?

  • The Big Mac Index is an accurate way of looking at PPP in the real world.

  • A Big Mac is going to be the same and hold the same intrinsic value no matter what country it is in, and so it is a good product to compare the currencies across countries.
